μ* — The Binary-Decimal Constant

The number \( \mu^* \) is defined as the infinite sum:

\[ \mu^* = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{f(n)}{10^n} \] where \( f(n) \) is the binary representation of \( n \), interpreted as a base-10 number.

A deeper analytic form can be derived as:

\[ \begin{align} \mu^* & =\sum_{a_k\in\{0, 1\}}^{k\geq0} \dfrac{\sum_{i\geq0} a_i10^i}{10^{\sum_{j\geq0} a_j2^j}} \\ & =\sum_{a_k\in\{0, 1\}}^{k\geq0} \sum_{i\geq0} a_i10^i\prod_{j\geq0} 10^{-a_j2^j} \\ & =\sum_{a_k\in\{0, 1\}}^{k\geq0} \sum_{i\geq0}a_i10^{i-a_i2^i}\prod_{j\geq0}^{i\neq j}10^{-a_j2^j} \\ & =\sum_{i\geq0}10^{i-2^i} \left(1+10^{-2^i}\right)^{-1}\left(1+10^{-2^0}\right) \left(1+10^{-2^1}\right) \left(1+10^{-2^2}\right)\cdots \\ & = \sum_{i\geq0}\dfrac{10^{i-2^i}}{1+10^{-2^i}}\cdot \left(1-10^{-2^0}\right)^{-1} \left(1-10^{-2^0}\right) \left(1+10^{-2^0}\right) \left(1+10^{-2^1}\right) \left(1+10^{-2^2}\right)\cdots \\ & = \dfrac{10}{9} \sum_{i\geq0} \dfrac{10^i}{10^{2^i}+1} \approx 0.222142213\cdots \end{align} \]

With the key contributions from Sangchul Lee and Claude Leibovici in Math SE.
