μ* — The Binary-Decimal Constant

The number \( \mu^* \) is defined as the infinite sum:

\[ \mu^* = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{f(n)}{10^n} \] where \( f(n) \) is the binary representation of \( n \), interpreted as a base-10 number.

It also has a remarkable closed-form expression(discovered by Sangchul Lee in Math SE):

\[ \mu^* = \frac{10}{9} \sum_{i=0}^\infty \frac{10^i}{10^{2^i} + 1} \] This identity, along with deeper analysis of its structure, was explored on Math Stack Exchange, with key contributions from Claude Leibovici and Sangchul Lee.
